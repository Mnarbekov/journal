**Status:** candidate — revised once

The original version (30 min, no email) was too narrow. Revised up to 45 minutes and broadened the constraint to cover all reactive-mode work: messages, requests, status updates, anything that's responding to someone else's agenda.

The first revision is captured above. This version extends the window and clarifies the intent: the goal isn't "inbox zero later in the day" — it's protecting the mental state you wake up with before the day starts shaping itself around other people's priorities.

Implementation notes:
- Turn off notifications before sitting down.
- Have the one task already chosen the night before (otherwise the 45 minutes gets spent deciding).
- Exceptions: genuine time-sensitive situations, but these are rarer than they feel in the moment.
